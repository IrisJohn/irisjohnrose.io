Wildlife Intrusion Detection

Introducing the Wildlife Intrusion Detection and Repulsion System: An initiative leveraging predictive modeling and computer vision to safeguard crops from animal intrusion. Utilizing IR sensors, the system detects animals within a 15-meter range, activating cameras for image capture. Employing machine learning, the system identifies the intruding animal, triggering an ultrasonic sound sensor that emits frequencies imperceptible to humans. This sound serves as a deterrent, repelling animals away from crops. The system, customized for Kerala's specific challenges with elephants and wild boars, achieved an initial accuracy of 50%, with ongoing potential for improvement through further processing. Additionally, an alert message is promptly sent to the respective owner, ensuring timely response and protection of valuable crops.

Assessing Predictive Models for Diabetes Diagnosis: A Comparative Algorithmic Analysis

Exploring the realm of diabetes prediction, this study leveraged a variety of machine learning algorithms such as Random Forest, Decision Tree, and k-Nearest Neighbors (kNN). The research focused on meticulously evaluating the accuracy of each algorithm, unraveling their distinctive strengths and weaknesses. By undertaking a rigorous assessment, the project aimed to unravel insights into the most effective predictive model for diabetes diagnosis. The findings contribute to the advancement of healthcare decision-making and personalized patient care in the realm of diabetes management
